# Important Takeaways

- **Courage vs Temperance**: Courage is the willingness to put your ass on the line — for something, for someone, for what you know you need to do. Selfl-discipline — the virtue of temperance — is even more important, the ability to keep your ass in line.
- **Self Discipline**:
  - Is giving everything you have… and knowing what to hold back.
  - Discipline isn’t deprivation… it brings rewards.
- Greatness is not just what one does, but also what one refuses to do.
- **Keep the main thing the main thing**: This means first, the discipline to step away and think: What am I doing? What are my priorities? What is the most important contribution I make — to my work, to my family, to the world?
- **Perfectionism is a vice**:
  - Because it’s never good enough. Because there’s always more they can do. Because it doesn’t measure up to what they did last time.
  - We can’t abandom a pursuit because we despair of perfecting it. Not trying because you’re not sure you can win, you’re not sure whether everyone will love it, there’s a word for that too: cowardice.
- **Getting better everyday**:
  - It’s a beautiful irony: You’re never content with your progress and yet, you’re always content… because you’re making progress.
  - It took about four hundred years for the Lord Jesus Christ to have his message accepted. Up to that time he would be considered a failure. As long as a man is trying as hard as he can to do what he thinks to be right, he is a success, regardless of outcome.
- **Tolerant with others strict with thyself**:
  - While we hold ourselves to the highest standards — and hope that our good behavior is contagious — we cannot expect everyone else to be like us. It’s not fair, nor is it possible.
- **Carry the load for others**:
  - A leader must be selfless, they must sacrifice, they must face the same deprivations as everyone else in the organization.
  - The leader shows up first and leaves last. The leader works hardest. The leader puts others before themselves. The leader takes the hit.
- **Self-discipline is virtue. Virtue is self-discipline**:
  - The relationship between temperance and the other three virtues of courage, justice and wisdom are impossible, worthless even, without self-discipline to bring them about.
  - The four virtues are about instilling character — good character — so that at the critical point, a person’s true nature kicks in. Self-discipline is not something that happens to you, it is something you cultivate.
