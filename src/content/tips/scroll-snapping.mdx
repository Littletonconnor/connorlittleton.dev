Scroll snapping is a relatively new feature that allows you to control where the viewport stops after a user scrolls. It's a great way to create a more engaging experience for your users. In this recipe, we'll explore how easy it is these days to implement scroll snapping.

## The markup

```html
<div class="mx-auto max-w-md px-8">
  <div
    class="edge-mask -mx-8 grid min-h-[350px] max-w-3xl snap-x snap-mandatory grid-cols-[repeat(4,100%)] gap-4 overflow-auto px-8 py-4"
  >
    <div
      class="snap-center snap-always rounded-2xl border bg-white p-6 shadow-md"
    >
      <h3 class="mb-2 text-xl font-semibold text-gray-800">Card title 1</h3>
      <p class="text-gray-600">Card description</p>
    </div>
    <div
      class="snap-center snap-always rounded-2xl border bg-white p-6 shadow-md"
    >
      <h3 class="mb-2 text-xl font-semibold text-gray-800">Card title 2</h3>
      <p class="text-gray-600">Card description</p>
    </div>
    <div
      class="snap-center snap-always rounded-2xl border bg-white p-6 shadow-md"
    >
      <h3 class="mb-2 text-xl font-semibold text-gray-800">Card title 3</h3>
      <p class="text-gray-600">Card description</p>
    </div>
    <div
      class="snap-center snap-always rounded-2xl border bg-white p-6 shadow-md"
    >
      <h3 class="mb-2 text-xl font-semibold text-gray-800">Card title 4</h3>
      <p class="text-gray-600">Card description</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
```

- Checkout this [Tailwind play link](https://play.tailwindcss.com/kRQDAaTNHp) to see it in action!

Scroll snapping simply consists of the following:

- A container with `scroll-snap-type: x mandatory` to enable scroll snapping on the x-axis.
- A child element with `scroll-snap-align: center` to align the child element to the center of the viewport when scrolling stops.
- A child element with `scroll-snap-stop: always` to ensure that the viewport stops at the child element.
- A container with overflow set to auto or scroll.
